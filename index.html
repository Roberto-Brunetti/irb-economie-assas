Research ethics

Ethical review of research in economics

The Institutional Review Board (IRB) in Economics of Université Paris Panthéon-Assas reviews the ethics of research projects carried out by the university's economists and issues advisory opinions on them.

Mission

The IRB's remit covers research involving human participants in particular: laboratory or field experiments, surveys, interviews, and the analysis of behavioural data.

Referral to the IRB is at the researcher's own initiative and the opinions issued are advisory. The IRB may also take up, on its own initiative, ethical questions raised by the work of the university's economists (good practice, methodological notes).

Review by the IRB does not exempt researchers from the referrals required by law to the relevant national authorities. The IRB is approved by the university's research council.

Submitting a project

Referring to the IRB

Referral is open to economics researchers at Université Paris Panthéon-Assas: faculty members, researchers, research engineers, doctoral and post-doctoral researchers, and temporary teaching and research assistants (ATER). For Master's projects, the referral is made by the dissertation supervisor.

Required documents

  • The completed submission form
  • The detailed research protocol
  • The participant information sheet
  • The informed consent form
  • Where applicable, permission to record audio or video
  • Where applicable, materials relating to GDPR compliance
  • Any other document relevant to the ethical review

Process

  1. Submission The form and supporting documents are sent to the IRB's public email address.
  2. Acknowledgement A file number (IRBASSAS-YYYY-NNN) is assigned and an acknowledgement is sent within 5 working days.
  3. Reviewers Two reviewers (anonymous to the applicant) are appointed within 10 working days.
  4. Review A draft opinion is prepared within 2 weeks, followed by members' observations and vote.
  5. Final opinion Issued by the chair within four weeks of receiving the complete file at the latest.

The decisions

Categories of opinion

The IRB issues one of the following four opinions.

Favourable

The project meets the ethical requirements; the research may be carried out as it stands.

Favourable with conditions

Compliant in principle, subject to clarifications or minor changes notified in writing and verified by the reviewers.

Unfavourable with revision

Substantial changes are required. A revised version may be submitted for a new review.

Unfavourable

As it stands, the project raises ethical issues that revision cannot resolve.

The same project may not be submitted more than three times to the committee. Minor changes to an already approved project are handled through a simplified procedure.
